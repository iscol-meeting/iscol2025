Call for Papers

The 2025 Israeli Seminar on Computational Linguistics (ISCOL 2025) invites presentations of recent work in all areas of natural language processing, computational linguistics and closely related fields. We accept work underway, provided that it represents recent and original work of interest to our audience. Submitted papers do not need to be anonymous.

We accept two types of submissions:

  1. Extended abstracts (up to 2 pages, not including references).
  2. Full papers that were submitted to other related venues. Please indicate at the end of the abstract field in the submission form whether the paper was accepted (and in this case, to which venue), or is currently under review.

FAQ

What is ISCOL?

ISCOL is the Israel Seminar on Computational Linguistics, a community venue for NLP and CL research. Details for 2025 are TBD.

Is ISCOL archival?

No. Works presented at ISCOL will not be considered as archived under ISCOL.

Can I submit a paper that was published or accepted to be published in another venue?

Yes. Preferably if it was accepted (to be) published during 2024 and was not presented in previous ISCOL events.

Can I submit a full paper instead of an extended abstract?

Yes. Please indicate whether the paper was accepted (and venue), or is currently under review. A mature work (in progress) that has not yet been submitted to any venue is also fine.

What are the submission file format and style?

You should submit a PDF file, preferably using a *CL format (Overleaf template, Latex and MS Word templates).

Will I need to print my poster?

Yes. You will have to print your own posters, hang it in the break before your poster session and take it down during the break following your poster session.

What are the dimensions for the posters?

The dimensions of the poster should be A0 (84.1 cm x 118.9 cm or 33.1 inches x 46.8 inches).

What is the event language?

English. All presentations will be in English.

How do I contact the organizers?

Contact us at iscol.pc@gmail.com or follow us on X (Twitter): @iscol_meeting.
